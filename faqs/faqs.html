What is a phishing scam? +
A phishing scam is a fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data by posing as a trustworthy entity through electronic communication such as email, text message, or phone call.
How do I recognize a phishing scam? +
Phishing scams can be recognized by looking for red flags such as suspicious sender addresses, typos or grammatical errors, urgent or threatening language, and requests for personal or financial information. The emails may also include links to fake websites or attachments containing malware.
What should I do if I receive a phishing email? +
Do not respond to the email or click on any links or attachments. Instead, report the email to the relevant authorities, such as your company's IT department or the Anti-Phishing Working Group. You should also delete the email from your inbox and trash folder.
